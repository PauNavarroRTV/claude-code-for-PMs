## Executive Summary

White Label OTT Apps (WLA) is Rakuten TV Enterprise's fully configurable, multi-device streaming platform, delivering premium branded experiences, seamless content discovery, robust playback, and flexible monetisation — all built with enterprise-grade stability, compliance, and scalability.

Designed and developed by in-house CTV engineers and UX experts, it combines a modular, integration-ready architecture with accessibility and proven UI/UX patterns, enabling partners to launch, manage, and grow their video services efficiently across Connected TV (Smart TV, STB, Roku, Console, and more), web, and mobile devices.

WLA is front-end first by design: it focuses on the viewer-facing experience — UI, layout, navigation, and interaction — while integrating seamlessly with partners' existing back-end systems including CMS, ad tech, analytics, and payments.

Backed by 16+ years of streaming expertise, proven across 150 million devices and 71 million remote control integrations across Europe.

**One-sentence summary:** White Label OTT Apps is a fully configurable, multi-device streaming platform delivering premium branded experiences, personalised content discovery, seamless monetisation, and enterprise-grade scalability — empowering partners to launch, manage, and grow their video services efficiently across web, mobile, and Connected TV.

## Monetisation Models

- **AVOD** — Advertising-based video on demand
- **FAST** — Free ad-supported streaming TV (live channels)
- **SVOD** — Subscription video on demand
- **TVOD** — Transactional video on demand (rent/buy/PPV)
- **Hybrid** — Any combination of the above in a single platform

## Section 8 — Viewer Psychology & Behavioral Expectations

### 8.1 WLA Viewer Dimension Taxonomy

#### Access & Entry
| Dimension | Definition |
|---|---|
| Entry Mode | How viewers arrive: search, EPG, recommendation rail, deep link, notification |
| Discovery Mode | Algorithmic, editorial, intentional (direct search) |
| Intent Strength | Casual (browsing) vs. explicit goal (specific title or event) |
| Access Friction | Registration gates, paywall steps, setup complexity |
| Time-to-Value | How fast the viewer expects to reach playback |
| Trigger Type | Habit, boredom, urgency (live event), fandom |

#### Attention & Cognitive
| Dimension | Definition |
|---|---|
| Attention Style | Focused, fragmented, ambient/background |
| Lean-In vs. Lean-Back | Active engagement vs. passive consumption |
| Scroll/Skip Propensity | Likelihood to abandon content or skip ads quickly |
| Retention Sensitivity | Drop-off risk on weak openings or playback delays |

#### Temporal
| Dimension | Definition |
|---|---|
| Session Length | Typical consumption duration per sitting |
| Viewing Cadence | Binge, episodic, appointment, sporadic |
| Time Sensitivity | Whether missing the moment degrades the value (live vs. evergreen) |
| Urgency Level | Immediate consumption need vs. deferred |
| Completion Expectation | Need to finish what was started |
| Continuity Expectation | Resume, watchlist, progress persistence across sessions and devices |
| Replay Likelihood | Probability of rewatching — affects download and buy decisions |

#### Monetization Psychology
| Dimension | Definition |
|---|---|
| Ad Tolerance | Acceptance of pre-roll, mid-roll, ad pods |
| Free Expectation | Assumption that content should be free |
| Subscription Justification | Value threshold required to commit to recurring payment |
| Transaction Sensitivity | Sensitivity to one-time purchase decisions |
| Ownership Expectation | Desire for permanent access (buy/EST vs. rent) |
| Premium Experience Expectation | Quality bar raised proportionally to price paid |

#### Platform Experience
| Dimension | Definition |
|---|---|
| UX Friction Tolerance | Patience for poor navigation, slow load, unclear UI |
| Error Tolerance | Acceptance of buffering, crashes, playback failures |
| Latency Sensitivity | Sensitivity to stream delay — critical for live |
| Recommendation Trust | Trust in algorithmic and editorial content rails |
| Personalization Expectation | Expected level of tailored content surface |
| Cross-Device Continuity | Expectation of seamless transition across TV, mobile, tablet |
| Playback Reliability Expectation | Uninterrupted delivery treated as a given, not a feature |

### 8.2 Content Type Baseline: Live vs. VOD

| Dimension | Live | VOD |
|---|---|---|
| Entry intent | Event-driven ("something is happening now") | Appetite-driven ("I want to watch something") |
| Urgency | High — missing it means missing it | Zero — content waits |
| Lean-in vs. lean-back | Lean-in (active, alert) | Lean-back (passive, relaxed) |
| Attention style | Focused, present-moment | Multi-episode autopilot |
| Session length | Matches broadcast duration | Self-determined — 22 min to 2h+ |
| Seek/scrub behaviour | Not expected | Core expectation — scrubbing, chapter skip, smart resume non-negotiable |
| Error tolerance | Very low — buffering during a live moment is brand-damaging | Moderate — rebuffering is annoying but recoverable |
| Primary device | CTV dominant | Multi-device |
| Loyalty driver | Rights ownership | Catalogue depth + recommendation quality |

### 8.3 Behavioral Profiles by Monetization Mode

#### FAST / AVOD — Free, Ad-Supported

**Viewer contract:** "I pay with attention. You give me free content."

| Dimension | Live FAST | VOD AVOD |
|---|---|---|
| Intent strength | Low — casual grazing | Low-medium — cost-conscious, "good enough free" |
| Ad tolerance | Moderate-High — conditioned by broadcast TV norms | Moderate — accepts pre-roll and mid-roll |
| Ad experience expectation | TV-style pods (2–3 ads per break). Countdown timer is a high-value UX retention element | 15–30s pre-rolls tolerated. 2 mid-rolls per 30-min episode is the ceiling |
| Access friction tolerance | Very low — paywall or registration gates are a hard stop | Low — account creation acceptable only if it unlocks clear upside |
| Error tolerance | Low — ad pod failure triggers immediate churn | Low-medium |
| Loyalty | Low — commodity | Low-medium |
| **Key failure moments** | Ad pod fails → blank screen → churn | Pre-roll loops / frequency cap failure → rage quit |

**Frontend requirements:**
- Ad countdown timer on live is a retention mechanism — must be in FD scope
- FAST EPG must be the primary navigation paradigm on CTV
- SSAI is required to prevent blank screen on ad failure
- Frequency capping must be specified in FDs, not delegated to ad-ops

---

#### SVOD — Subscription

**Viewer contract:** "I pay a fixed fee. I own my viewing schedule."

| Dimension | Live SVOD | VOD SVOD |
|---|---|---|
| Intent strength | High — destination behaviour | Medium-high — catalogue-motivated |
| Ad tolerance | Zero — has paid to escape ads | Zero on base tier |
| Error tolerance | Low — buffering during key live moment is disproportionately rage-inducing | Medium |
| Churn triggers | Rights loss, stream quality degradation | Catalogue exhaustion, UI regression, price increase |
| Premium experience expectation | 4K/HDR on big events, LL-HLS/LL-DASH | 4K/HDR on flagship, seamless resume, skip intro/recap, downloads |
| **Key failure moments** | Buffer during a goal or crucial moment | Catalogue exhaustion with no recovery path |

**Frontend requirements:**
- Live SVOD requires LL-HLS or LL-DASH
- DVR/catchup window must be scoped in FDs
- Skip intro, smart resume, and autoplay next are baseline
- Profile management and parental controls are expected as standard

---

#### TVOD / PPV — Transactional

**Viewer contract:** "I paid specifically for this. Everything must be perfect."

| Dimension | Live PPV | VOD TVOD (rent/buy) |
|---|---|---|
| Intent strength | Maximum — event-driven, high anticipation | High — deliberate purchasing decision |
| Ad tolerance | Zero. Non-negotiable | Near-zero |
| Error tolerance | Effectively zero — a buffer during a KO triggers immediate refund demand | Very low |
| Access friction tolerance | Checkout must be ≤3 clicks to confirmed purchase | Same — clear pricing, transparent rental window |
| Premium experience expectation | Best possible — 4K/HDR, lowest achievable latency | 4K/HDR standard. Offline download expected for Buy (EST) |
| **Key failure moments** | Single buffer on the knockout. No visible refund path | Rental expired before watching. 4K unavailable |

**Frontend requirements:**
- Checkout flow: 3-click maximum to confirmed purchase
- "My Purchases" / "My Events" must be a persistent, prominent UI section
- Rental expiry countdown must surface on the content card, not only inside the player
- Pre-event page (countdown, undercard content) is a retention and upsell surface

### 8.4 Emma Mindset by Mode

| Mode | Mindset | Trigger to Leave |
|---|---|---|
| FAST Live | "Free TV, good enough, I'm browsing" | Ad breaks too long, stream dies, nothing interesting |
| AVOD VOD | "Free is fine, I'll tolerate ads for this" | Pre-roll loops, mid-roll too frequent, poor recommendation |
| SVOD Live | "I pay for this — I own this moment" | Buffer during the key moment, no DVR, quality drops |
| SVOD VOD | "This is my comfort zone, I live here" | Ran out of things to watch, UI regressed, price went up |
| PPV Live | "I paid £30 for this fight. Don't ruin it." | Single buffer event, no refund path, checkout was confusing |
| TVOD VOD | "I chose this deliberately. Treat it like cinema." | Rental expired before watching, 4K unavailable, no download |

### 8.5 Frontend Decision Shortcuts by Monetization Mode

| Mode | Non-negotiable frontend requirements |
|---|---|
| FAST | EPG as primary CTV navigation, SSAI, ad countdown timer, frequency cap config |
| AVOD | SSAI, pre-roll/mid-roll config, frequency cap, lightweight registration gate |
